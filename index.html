General Information

What is the coronavirus?
Coronaviruses are a family of viruses that affect mammals and birds. They can cause respiratory infections in humans, with severities ranging from mild to lethal.

What are some examples of coronaviruses?
Notable examples of members of this family include:

  • The common cold. Mild symptoms.
  • Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS-CoV). Outbreak in 2003. Potentially lethal.
  • Middle East Respiratory Syndrome (MERS-CoV). Outbreak in 2012. Potentially lethal.
  • Novel Coronavirus (2019-nCoV). Ongoing outbreak. Potentially lethal.


What is the Novel Coronavirus?
The Novel Coronavirus, or more commonly referred to in the media simply as "coronavirus", is a coronavirus originating in the capital of Hubei in China, Wuhan. It was discovered when a multitude of citizens started to display symptoms of a disease like pneumonia; however, doctors were unable to treat it, nor were they able to identify the cause. It is hypothesized to be a zoonotic disease, having originated from victims consuming animals such as bats.

Causes, Symptoms, and Countermeasures

Transmissions have been confirmed to occur between humans via breathing, coughing, or sneezing. Those who have visited Mainland China recently may be at risk. Elderly people and people with pre-existing medical conditions are more susceptible to 2019-nCoV.

What are some symptoms?
Symptoms will typically start to appear anytime from two days to two weeks after contracting the virus. Some signs of the virus include:

  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Shortness of Breath


Individuals are advised to wear proper face masks to prevent the spread of the disease. If you suspect that you may have the virus, contact a medical professional through the telephone, not in person.
